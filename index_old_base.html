Core Beliefs

On Uncertainty & Information

Wager on hope P(good|a=bad) = 0 +- \epsilon P(good|a=good) > 0 +- \epsilon (strictly greater than zero) good = argmax_{a}P(outcome = good|a \in action) action = {good, bad} with epsilon accounting for natural randomness - have the audacity to believe that things can be better

Feelings aren't good or bad—they're informative sensors signaling when change is needed. The existence of emotion itself is profound. We find balance in responding to the signals of the world around us, through deliberate analysis and simply being present in the moment.

Intentional Actions

Innovation is awesome. And dangerous.

We should design our future deliberately, not stumble into it. Always ask:

  • How beneficial to humanity is it?
  • What are the intended consequences?
  • What are the potential hidden, unintended consequences
  • Is it necessary? What problem does it solve?

Nothing should be taboo or too sacred for careful scrutiny If you can not understand something, you can not make meaningful decisions about it. This includes:

Things should be experienced -> there is beauty in scrutiny and in experience. Each has its appropriate time and place

Subsidise good intentions with well-built systems:

Make it easy to have good intentions and take action towards them. Do not rely on the presence of willpower, good intentions, and mental clarity Doing the right thing should be the path of least resistance

On Feedback & Growth

Being wrong is painful. Receiving feedback can be uncomfortable. But feedback is the mechanism by which we become less wrong over time. If you want to learn to juggle, you’re going to have to drop a few balls Receiving feedback is awesome. Choosing to provide feedback/pushback is hard: you choose to vocalize something that might be upsetting to the recipient is high action potential. Thank everyone who contributes to your loss function, then critically analyze what you agree with and act on verifiable insights.

If someone makes a claim, it almost always has some truth to it. Feedback can be conclusions tangled in emobtions → look for the underlying observations. Their conclusions may not be accurate, but their observations rarely lie. They said something because they saw something

On Disagreement & Understanding

Most disagreements are differences of information, not values. Changing someone's mind requires them to dismantle and reconstruct part of their worldview. To break down someone's worldview is to remove part of themselves. Be kind when doing so.

reframe other's goals to be aligned with your own. Understanding motives and shaping rewards is the fundamental skill: the ability to influence your enviornment To convince someone to take an action requires you to show them how that action moves them closer to one of their goals. And the higher priority that goal the sooner they will take said action*
