Core Beliefs

On Uncertainty & Action

Making decisions under uncertainty is a superpower. We're often terribly calibrated at estimating our own confidence, yet waiting for certainty guarantees inaction. Do it afraid. Get started. Action transforms possibility into reality. Being wrong is painful, but staying still out of fear of being wrong is worse—pain is information, and suffering happens when we deny ourselves that information.

On Feelings as Sensors

Feelings aren't good or bad—they're informative sensors signaling when change is needed. The existence of emotion itself is profound. We find balance in responding to the signals of the world around us, through deliberate analysis and simply being present in the moment.

On Optimism as Strategy

Optimism isn't naive—it's strategic. If you expect bad things and act accordingly, only bad outcomes are possible. But if you take constructive action, you create non-zero probability for good outcomes. You've fundamentally changed what's possible.

On Technology & Responsibility

We should design our future deliberately, not stumble into it. Technology should be developed with strong consideration for its usefulness and benefit to humanity—not solely because it's possible to build. Nothing should be taboo to careful scrutiny. If you cannot understand something, you cannot make meaningful decisions about it.

On Feedback & Growth

Receiving feedback is awesome—it's your signal for improvement. Thank everyone who gives it, then critically analyze what you agree with. When someone makes a claim, it almost always contains some truth. People give feedback as conclusions; your job is to look for the underlying observations. Their conclusions may be wrong, but their observations rarely are.

On Disagreement & Understanding

Most disagreements are differences of information, not values. Changing someone's mind requires them to dismantle and reconstruct part of their worldview—you're asking them to remove a piece of themselves. Be kind when doing so.

On Diversity of Thought

I'm thrilled that people are so different, with such diverse curiosities and interests. If the world were full of single-minded individuals, we'd lose everything that makes us whole. The collective intelligence of humanity depends on this diversity.

On Learning from Others

I cannot live every lifetime or explore every possibility. The best way to learn is to borrow from others' experiences as well as your own. For every human you encounter, there exists something you can learn from them.
